The origin of basketball

Basketball was invented in 1891 by Dr. James Naismith, the sports coach of YMCA Training School in Springfield, Massachusetts. At that time, in the cold winter, the lack of indoor sports ball games. Inspired by the game in which workers and children cast the ball to the "peach basket", Naismith designed to nail two peach baskets on the balustrades of the stands at both ends of the gymnasium. The opening of the peach basket is horizontally upward, 10 feet from the ground. He used football as a tool to throw the ball into the basket. One point was scored when he entered the basket, and the winner was decided by the score. Because the game was originally used as a peach basket and ball, it was named basketball. In 1883, the iron ball basket replaced the peach basket and put on the net. In 1895, the basket began to be fixed at 4 × In 1913, he cut the net to form a modern rebounding and basketball.

development

The supreme organization of basketball, the International Basketball Federation, was founded in 1932 and headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland. The highest level of basketball in the world is the NBA competition in the United States. The highest level of basketball in China is the CBA competition.

Position on the court

Positions 1 to 5 in the basketball court are respectively point guard, shooting guard, small forward, power forward and center. Each position is in different areas of the basketball court.

Rules

In the game, basketball can only be played with both hands. It can pass, throw, clap, roll or transport in any direction, but it can't run with the ball. It can't deliberately kick the ball or block the ball with any part of the leg or use the boxing ball.
